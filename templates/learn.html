Cyber Security 101

Learn the basics of cybersecurity, including concepts, threats, and best practices.

1. Introduction to Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. With the increase in online threats, organizations and individuals need to adopt strategies to ensure their security.

2. Types of Cybersecurity Threats

Below are the most common types of threats in cybersecurity:

  • Phishing: Tricking users into revealing sensitive information via fake emails.
  • Malware: Malicious software such as viruses, worms, and ransomware.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks: Intercepting communication between two parties.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS): Overloading systems to make them unavailable.

3. Key Cybersecurity Principles

Focus on the core principles to ensure security:

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring sensitive information is only accessible to authorized people.
  • Integrity: Ensuring data is accurate and has not been tampered with.
  • Availability: Ensuring systems and data are available when needed.

4. Best Practices for Cybersecurity

  • Use strong passwords with a mix of characters.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Install antivirus software and firewalls.
  • Regularly update software and systems.
  • Be cautious of phishing emails.
  • Backup important data regularly.

5. Cybersecurity Tools

Some widely used tools include:

  • Firewalls: Block unauthorized access to networks.
  • Antivirus Software: Detect and remove malware.
  • Password Managers: Securely store strong passwords.
  • Encryption Tools: Protect sensitive data.

6. Real-Life Examples of Cyberattacks

  • Yahoo Data Breach (2013-2014): Over 3 billion user accounts were compromised.
  • WannaCry Ransomware (2017): Affected over 200,000 systems worldwide.
  • Equifax Data Breach (2017): Exposed data of 147 million individuals.

7. Career Opportunities in Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity offers exciting career opportunities, such as:

  • Security Analyst
  • Penetration Tester
  • Incident Responder
  • Security Engineer
